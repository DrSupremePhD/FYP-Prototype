üîê 5. Privacy-Preserving Technology (PSI)

PrivaGene employs Private Set Intersection (PSI) technology, a cryptographic protocol that enables genetic risk assessment while maintaining bilateral privacy protection.

How PSI Protects Your Privacy

  1. Your Data Stays Private: Your genetic markers are encrypted before computation. Neither PrivaGene nor healthcare providers can see your raw genetic data.
  2. Hospital Data Stays Private: Disease-gene databases from hospitals are also protected.
  3. Only Results Are Revealed: The only output is a risk percentage based on the count of matching genes, not the identity of specific genes.
üßÆ Risk Calculation Formula

Risk% = c √ó |X ‚à© Y| / n

Where X = your encrypted gene set, Y = hospital's disease gene set, c = disease-specific constant, and n = total disease genes. Only the intersection COUNT is revealed, never the actual matching genes. This uses the Diffie-Hellman based PSI protocol.

What Researchers CANNOT Access

  • ‚ùå Raw genetic data or gene sets

  4. Audit Trails: All researcher data queries are logged
  5. User Consent: Research access requires explicit user opt-in
