Frequently Asked Questions

üîê Privacy & Security

How is my genetic data protected?

Your genetic data is encrypted using AES-256 encryption before upload. We use Private Set Intersection (PSI) to compute risk assessments without exposing your raw genetic information. Your data is never stored in plaintext.

Who can access my data?

Only you have access to your full genetic data and results. Hospitals and specialists can only see information you explicitly share with them during consultations. Researchers only access anonymized, aggregated data with your consent.

Can I delete my data?

Yes, you can delete your account and all associated data at any time from your profile settings. Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone.

üß¨ How PSI Works

What is Private Set Intersection (PSI)?

PSI is a cryptographic technique that allows two parties to find common elements in their datasets without revealing any other information. In PrivaGene, we use PSI to match your genetic markers against disease-associated genes in our database without exposing your raw genetic data.

How accurate are the risk assessments?

Our risk assessments are based on peer-reviewed genetic research and continuously updated gene databases. However, genetic risk is just one factor in disease development. Environmental factors, lifestyle, and family history also play important roles.

How long does risk assessment take?

PSI computation typically takes 2-5 minutes depending on the number of disease categories selected and the size of your genetic data file.

üì§ Uploading Data

What file formats are supported?

We accept genetic data in common formats: VCF, 23andMe, AncestryDNA, and standard CSV files with genetic markers. Maximum file size is 50MB.

Can I upload multiple files?

Yes, you can upload multiple genetic data files. Each upload is processed separately and tracked in your upload history.
